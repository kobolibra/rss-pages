What a difference a deal makes

来源：https://www.ib.barclays/our-insights/weekly-insights.html

Oil prices eased and risk assets soared in expectation of a US-Iran deal that would open the Strait of Hormuz. Central banks signaled 'no rush' in their policy responses and markets priced out aggressive rate hike scenarios. To justify this optimism, much now depends on the actual deal to deliver.

* **US:**With little incoming data, our Research team's read of US conditions is little changed this week entering the April FOMC blackout. Available data suggest activity remains resilient amid the Iran conflict, and that pass-through to core prices has been limited. The focus now shifts to March retail sales estimates.

* **Euro Area:**ECB speakers have increasingly coalesced around the need to gain further clarity on the evolution of the inflation shock before recalibrating monetary policy, thereby reducing the case for a move at the April meeting. Our Research analysts revise their call and now expect the ECB to raise rates in June and September.

* **United Kingdom:**Our Research analysts revise up their GDP forecast to reflect strong monthly data, but caution against over-interpreting the signal for underlying growth. They expect headline inflation to accelerate next week. Meanwhile, MPC speakers remained cautious in their guidance at the annual IMF meetings in Washington.

* **Japan:**Amid Middle East uncertainty and deteriorating sentiment, our Research team believes BoJ will find it difficult to assess prospects of/risks to its baseline outlook and decide on a hike by April. That said, its stance remains firm. Assuming uncertainty eases, our Research team still thinks it will hike in June.

* **China:**Better-than-expected Q1 GDP growth—driven by exports and infrastructure investment—has prompted our Reserach analysts to raise their 2026 growth forecast by 0.6pp to 4.6%. That said, a deteriorated labor market and reduced pro-consumption subsidy would drag consumption. A sustained recovery in housing remains uncertain.

* **Emerging Asia:**Our Research analysts do not believe the MAS's decision to tighten its monetary policy settings presages a wave of rate hikes through Emerging Asia – at least not in the near term. Our Research team expects BI and the BSP to remain on hold next week.

* **EEMEA:**The overall tone toward EMs was cautiously positive, despite lingering uncertainty around US–Iran peace talks. Even under a scenario of gradual moderating energy prices, inflation remains the key concern, keeping central banks firmly in wait-and-see mode.

* **Latin America:**Amid the troubled waters of geopolitical upheaval, Latin America’s outlook remains resilient. While energy shocks and political tensions persist, growth forecasts are steady. Brazil, Mexico, and Chile outperform, but Argentina and Colombia face headwinds. Electoral uncertainty remains a key risk.
